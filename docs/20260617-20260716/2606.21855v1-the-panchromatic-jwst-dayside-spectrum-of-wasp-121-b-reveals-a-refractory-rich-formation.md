## 摘要
理解行星形成的一条途径是将它们当前的大气成分与行星形成模型的预测联系起来。对于最热的行星，难挥发元素的丰度可以提供有用的形成示踪物，补充传统使用的C/O和整体金属丰度。这里我们将新的JWST MIRI/LRS观测与存档的NIRSpec/G395H和NIRISS/SOSS数据相结合，获得了0.6至12微米的全色昼侧发射光谱，研究超热木星WASP-121 b大气中的难挥发元素丰度。我们的反演分析高置信度地检测到了难挥发示踪物SiO气体，以及之前检测到的易挥发物种。该大气富含易挥发和难挥发物种，相对于ESPRESSO数据给出的新的恒星丰度约束，难挥发与易挥发比例增加：Si/O=3.54^{+0.86}_{-0.69}倍恒星值，Si/C=3.05^{+1.12}_{-0.80}倍恒星值。此外，我们确认了TiO的贫化以及需要附加的反射光不透明源，其几何反照率为0.22±0.03。反演的昼侧温度廓线具有强烈的逆温层，其结构比标准参数化所能容纳的更为复杂，食图分析表明存在一个小幅度的东向热点偏移，偏移量为4.8^{+2.7°}_{-2.8}。将我们的结果与行星形成模型进行比较，我们发现测得的富集模式是由多个储层的吸积塑造的，要么是通过水冰线内部的固体和气体吸积混合物，要么是在从盘更远处向内迁移过程中持续的固体吸积。最后，我们对行星的动力学历史进行建模，发现它可能由于形成后的动力学事件（如行星-行星散射或von Zeipel-Lidov-Kozai循环）而达到当前的高倾角轨道。

## Abstract
One path to understand how planets form is to link their present-day atmospheric composition to predictions from planet formation models. For the hottest planets, the abundances of refractory species can provide a useful formation tracer, complementing the traditionally used C/O and overall metallicity. Here we investigate the refractory abundance in the atmosphere of the ultra-hot Jupiter WASP-121 b, combining new JWST MIRI/LRS observations with archival NIRSpec/G395H and NIRISS/SOSS data to obtain a panchromatic dayside emission spectrum from 0.6 to 12 $μ$m. Our retrieval analysis detects the refractory tracer SiO gas at high confidence, in addition to previously detected volatile species. The atmosphere is enriched in volatile and refractory species, with enhanced refractory-to-volatile ratios of Si/O=$3.54^{+0.86}_{-0.69}$x stellar and Si/C=$3.05^{+1.12}_{-0.80}$x stellar, relative to new stellar abundance constraints from ESPRESSO data. In addition, we confirm the depletion of TiO and the need for an additional source of reflected light opacity with a geometric albedo of $0.22\pm0.03$. The retrieved dayside temperature profile has a strong inversion layer, with a more complex structure than standard parameterizations can accommodate, and an eclipse map analysis indicates a small eastward hotspot offset of $4.8^{+2.7\circ}_{-2.8}$. Comparing our results with models of planet formation, we find that the measured enrichment pattern was shaped by accretion from multiple reservoirs, either through a mixture of solid and gas accretion interior to the water ice line or through continued solid accretion during inward migration from farther out in the disk. Finally, we model the planet's dynamical history and find that it could reach its current high-obliquity orbit as a consequence of a post-formation dynamical event, such as planet-planet scattering or von Zeipel-Lidov-Kozai cycles.
