## 摘要
研究表明，木星的极光由该行星最内侧卫星伊奥的火山喷发所提供的等离子体维持。围绕近十几个孤立亚恒星世界探测到的重复射电爆发被认为是大规模放大的木星极光类似物，但为极光提供电子的来源仍然未知。潮汐加热的系外卫星上的火山活动可能为这些世界的极光提供等离子体。我们评估凌星观测是否为探测极光活跃的亚恒星世界周围的系外卫星提供了可行手段，从而能够对该假设进行未来测试。具体而言，我们分析了SIMP 0136+0933（一颗12.7倍木星质量的“超级木星”，已知显示极光发射）的JWST近红外和中红外光变曲线。我们展示了在SIMP 0136+0933系统中探测与木星伽利略卫星质量比相当的系外卫星的能力，对于伊奥-木星质量比卫星的探测成功率达到66%，对于木卫三-木星质量比卫星达到93%。尽管现有的光变曲线足以证明该技术能够探测凌星系外卫星，但可用的档案数据持续时间太短，无法对该系统中是否存在凌星卫星施加有意义的约束。我们得出结论，对4至12颗已知极光活跃的超级木星进行约1.5天的JWST光变曲线观测，将足以提供支持或反对该假设的证据。小目标样本可能就足够了，因为卫星周期短会增加凌星概率，而极光活跃的世界可能优先在接近侧向倾角的情况下被观测到。

## Abstract
Studies suggest Jupiter's aurorae are supplied with plasma from volcanic outflows on the planet's innermost moon, Io. Repeating bursts of radio emission thought to trace massively scaled-up analogs of Jupiter's aurorae have been detected around nearly a dozen isolated substellar worlds, yet the source of the electrons fueling the aurorae remains unknown. Volcanism from tidally heated exosatellites may provide the plasma that fuel the aurora on these worlds. We assess whether transit observations provide a viable means of detecting exosatellites around aurorally active substellar worlds, thereby enabling future tests of this hypothesis. Specifically, we analyze JWST near- and mid-infrared light curves of SIMP 0136+0933, a $12.7 M_J$ "super-Jupiter", known to exhibit auroral emission. We demonstrate the capability to detect exosatellites in the SIMP 0136+0933 system with satellite-to-host mass ratios comparable to those of Jupiter's Galilean moons, achieving detection success rates of 66% for Io-to-Jupiter mass ratio satellites and 93% for Ganymede-to-Jupiter mass ratio satellites. Although the existing light curve is sufficient to demonstrate that this technique is capable of detecting transiting exosatellites, the available archival data are too short in duration to place meaningful constraints on the presence of a transiting satellite in this system. We conclude that JWST light curves spanning $\sim$1.5 days for 4-12 known aurorally active super-Jupiters would be sufficient to yield evidence for or against this hypothesis. A small target sample may suffice, as short satellite periods boost transit probabilities and aurorally active worlds may be preferentially observed near edge-on inclinations.
