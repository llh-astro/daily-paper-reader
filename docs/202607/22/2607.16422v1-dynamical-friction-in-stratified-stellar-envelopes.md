## 摘要
常用于公共包层和行星吞没旋进的动力学摩擦公式通常假设均匀介质和/或直线扰动体运动。然而，嵌入在巨星包层中的引力体在通过有限径向分层介质沿弯曲轨道运动时，会激发轨道尺度的尾流。我们制定了在流体静力学、球对称分层气体介质中，处于圆轨道上的弱点扰动的线性正压声学响应。我们将该公式应用于幂律密度分布和巨星包层模型中的单扰动体，以及幂律密度背景下的双扰动体。我们发现分层通过尾流的整体结构影响动力学摩擦。径向分量由低阶、轨道尺度的尾流决定，其振幅和符号可能与均匀介质结果有显著差异。方位角分量也受到分层的修正，但在超音速状态下，它保留了均匀问题中的库仑对数敏感性。在双扰动体系统中，伴星尾流可以显著改变给定组件的径向力并减少方位角阻力，但与扰动体自身的尾流不同，它没有局部的库仑对数贡献。对于所采用的巨星包层剖面，分层尾流施加的方位角阻力给出的旋进时间比用相同局部背景量评估的均匀介质公式更短。该公式提供了一个灵活的工具，用于在指定的径向分层中计算嵌入扰动体的尾流，并且是朝着计算高效、自洽的公共包层和行星吞没旋进模型迈出的第一步。

## Abstract
Dynamical friction prescriptions used for common-envelope and planetary engulfment inspirals often assume a homogeneous medium and/or rectilinear perturber motion. A gravitating object embedded in a giant-star envelope instead excites an orbit-scale wake while moving on a curved orbit through a finite, radially stratified medium. We formulate the linear barotropic acoustic response of a weak point perturber on a circular orbit in a hydrostatic, spherically stratified gaseous medium. We apply the formulation to single perturbers in power-law density profiles and giant-star envelope models, and to double perturbers in power-law density backgrounds. We find that stratification affects dynamical friction through the global structure of the wake. The radial component is set by the low-order, orbit-scale wake and can strongly differ in amplitude and sign from the homogeneous-medium result. The azimuthal component is also modified by stratification, but in the supersonic regime it retains the Coulomb-logarithmic sensitivity of the homogeneous problem. In double-perturber systems, the companion wake can substantially change the radial force and reduce the azimuthal drag on a given component, but, unlike the perturber's own wake, it has no local Coulomb-logarithmic contribution. For the adopted giant-star envelope profiles, the azimuthal drag exerted by the stratified wake gives shorter inspiral times than uniform-medium prescriptions evaluated with the same local background quantities. The formulation provides a flexible tool for computing embedded-perturber wakes in prescribed radial stratifications and is a first step toward computationally efficient, self-consistent models of common-envelope and planetary-engulfment inspirals.
