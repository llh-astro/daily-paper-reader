## 摘要
碎片盘的观测有可能为我们提供关于大质量行星通过引力扰动它们的有价值信息。在这项工作中，我们探讨了受内行星长期扰动的盘中方位角平均（或轴对称）面密度（ASD）——一种常规从观测中推导出的特征——的演化。我们发展了ASD演化的详细解析理解，并使用一个新的数值框架DebrisPy（我们已公开提供）进行验证。利用这些工具，我们表明，在长期演化的盘中，ASD会形成一组尖锐特征——在离心率零点处出现弱不连续的峰值，以及在粒子近心点或远心点堆积的焦散点处出现尖锐的不连续性——随着盘的老化向外推进。我们探究了这些特征对行星离心率、自由与强迫粒子离心率之比以及底层径向质量分布的依赖性，特别表明更偏心的行星会产生更显著的ASD特征。与真实观测的点扩散函数（以及碎片非零的随机自由离心率）卷积会平滑这些特征，但在高分辨率观测中仍可探测。长期ASD峰的径向位置遵循特定的、定义明确的模式，这应在观测中明确指向它们的长期性质。我们说明了在观测到的盘中探测到或未探测到这种长期特征如何（结合其他约束）用于测量或约束扰动行星的关键参数（甚至那些尚未被探测到的）——质量、半长轴和离心率。

## Abstract
Observations of debris discs have the potential to provide us with valuable information about massive planets perturbing them gravitationally. In this work, we explore the evolution of the azimuthally-averaged (or axisymmetric) surface density (ASD) -- a characteristic routinely derived from observations -- in a disc secularly perturbed by an inner planet. We develop detailed analytical understanding of ASD evolution and verify it using a novel numerical framework DebrisPy, which we make publicly available. With these tools we show that in a secularly evolving disc ASD develops a set of sharp features -- weakly discontinuous peaks at eccentricity nulls and sharp discontinuities at caustic points where particle periastra or apoastra pile up -- marching out through the disc as it ages. We probe the dependence of these features on planetary eccentricity, ratio of the free to forced particle eccentricity, and underlying radial mass distribution, showing in particular that more eccentric planets produce more prominent ASD features. Convolution with the PSF of realistic observations (as well as the non-zero random free eccentricity of debris) smooths out these features, but they can still be detectable in high-resolution observations. Radial locations of secular ASD peaks follow a particular, well-defined pattern, which should unambiguously point to their secular nature in observations. We illustrate how both detection and non-detection of such secular features in observed discs can be used (in combination with other constraints) to measure or constrain key parameters of perturbing planets (even those not yet detected) -- mass, semi-major axis and eccentricity.
