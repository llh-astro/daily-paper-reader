## 摘要
周期在20 < P < 300天范围内、环绕类太阳恒星的气态巨行星是行星形成中相对罕见的结果，关于这一子群的性质和形成的关键问题仍未解答。关于其形成位置（原位或异位）以及后续迁移的理论模型预测了不同的行星质量和偏心率结果，表明观测在揭示其历史中具有关键作用。在这项工作中，我们展示了一对长周期木星大小行星凌星一颗F7恒星的发现与确认：TOI-791 b 是一个轨道周期为139.29931_{-0.00012}^{+0.00011}天的0.993±0.033 R_Jup行星，TOI-791 c 是一个轨道周期为232.01570_{-0.00071}^{+0.00067}天的1.155±0.040 R_Jup行星。这两颗行星处于二阶5:3周期共振的0.07%之内，导致凌星时间变化（TTVs）高达50分钟。我们利用地面测光确认了它们的行星性质，包括从南极ASTEP对TOI-791 b和c的超过11小时凌星的多次完整探测，这使它们成为有史以来从地面完全观测到的持续时间最长的凌星。我们对TTV信号的详细分析使我们能够测量两颗行星的动力学质量，得到密度ρ_b=0.038±0.008 g cm^{-3}和ρ_c=0.047±0.006 g cm^{-3}，表明TOI-791 b和c是迄今为止探测到的密度最低的巨行星中的两个。虽然这些测量结果是可靠的，但需要进一步后续观测来全面表征TTV信号和系统结构。

## Abstract
Gas giant planets with periods $20~<~P~<~300~\rm days$ orbiting Sun-like stars are a relatively uncommon outcome of planetary formation, and key questions about the nature and formation of this sub-population remain unanswered. Theoretical models for the location of their formation (in- or ex-situ) and for their subsequent migration predict different outcomes in terms of planet masses and eccentricities, indicating that observations have a key role to play in disentangling their histories. In this work we present the discovery and confirmation of a pair of long-period Jupiter-sized planets transiting an F7 star: TOI-791 b is a $0.993\pm0.033\rm~R_{Jup}$ planet on a $139.29931_{-0.00012}^{+0.00011}~\rm day$ orbit, and TOI-791 c, a $1.155\pm0.040\rm ~R_{Jup}$ planet on a $232.01570_{-0.00071}^{+0.00067}~\rm day$ orbit. The two planets are within 0.07% of a second-order 5:3 period commensurability leading to transit timing variations (TTVs) of up to 50 minutes. We confirm their planetary nature using ground-based photometry, including multiple full detections of the $>11~\rm hr$ transits of both TOI-791 b and c from Antarctica with ASTEP, making these the longest-duration transits ever observed in their entirety from the ground. Our detailed analysis of the TTV signal allows us to measure dynamical masses for both planets, which yield densities of $ρ_{\rm b}=0.038\pm0.008 \rm ~g~cm^{-3}$ and $ρ_{\rm c}=0.047\pm0.006 \rm ~g~cm^{-3}$, indicating that TOI-791~b and c are two of the lowest density giant planets ever detected. While these measurements are robust, further follow-up is needed to fully characterise the TTV signal and the architecture of the system.
