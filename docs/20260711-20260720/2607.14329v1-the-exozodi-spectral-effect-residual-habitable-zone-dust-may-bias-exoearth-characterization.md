## 摘要
所有系外行星系统预计都存在来自星子的系外黄道尘埃（即系外黄道光）。对于许多恒星而言，系外黄道光可能是直接观测类地系外行星时最大的天体物理噪声源。邻近的类太阳系统宜居带尘埃含量可能比我们太阳系高约3倍，这些尘埃必须从直接图像和光谱中去除才能揭示系外行星信号。这种微米级尘埃的反照率在可见光到近红外波段平滑变化，但系外黄道光可由不同物质组成，从而影响其颜色。若未扣除，系外黄道光将向提取的光谱添加类似云的连续谱辐射，可能通过降低光谱特征的视吸收深度而使表征研究产生偏差。为量化这些效应，我们模拟了具有不同系外黄道光密度和成分的类地系外行星系统，并将大气反演工具应用于合成宜居世界天文台（HWO）光谱。我们发现，与太阳系相当（即1个黄道单位）的系外黄道光可将可见光波长分子吸收特征的视深度降低高达50%，且该效应在较长波长处更为严重。为测量分子丰度，可能需要大量后处理将系外黄道光去除到残余分数随尘埃密度而收紧的程度。然而，针对吸收物种的二元探测结果则可将此要求放宽一个数量级，尤其是在较高光谱分辨率下。理解并减轻系外黄道光对提取的类地系外行星光谱的影响，对于利用HWO表征宜居带系外行星环境，并最终寻找宜居性和生命迹象至关重要。

## Abstract
All exoplanetary systems are expected to host exozodiacal dust, or exozodi, originating from planetesimals. For many stars, exozodi will likely be the largest source of astrophysical noise in direct observations of terrestrial exoplanets. Nearby Sun-like systems likely have $\gtrsim3$x more habitable zone (HZ) dust than our solar system, which must be removed from direct images and spectra to reveal exoplanet signals. The albedo of this micron-sized dust varies smoothly over VIS -- NIR wavelengths, but exozodi can be composed of different materials that can impact its color. If left unsubtracted, exozodiacal light will add cloud-like continuum emission to extracted spectra, potentially biasing characterization studies by reducing the apparent absorption depth of spectral features. To quantify these effects, we simulate exoEarth systems with a range of exozodi densities and compositions, and apply an atmospheric retrieval tool to synthetic Habitable Worlds Observatory (HWO) spectra. We find that exozodi at levels similar to the solar system (i.e., 1 zodi) can reduce the apparent depth of visible wavelength molecular absorption features by up to 50\%, an effect that worsens at longer wavelengths. To measure molecular abundances, significant post-processing may be required to remove exozodi to a fractional residual that tightens with dust density. However, targeting a binary detection result for an absorbing species instead relaxes this requirement by an order of magnitude, especially at higher spectral resolution. Understanding and mitigating the effects of exozodi in extracted exoEarth spectra is critical to characterize HZ exoplanet environments with HWO and ultimately to search for signs of habitability and life.
