## 摘要
HD 202206系统包含两颗亚恒星伴星，其周期比为5:1，围绕一颗类太阳恒星运行，这为研究高阶平运动共振提供了难得的机会，并为系外行星系统中亚恒星伴星的形成与演化提供了新见解。我们重新审视了HD 202206系统，旨在对轨道倾角、伴星质量、共振动力学和潜在形成机制进行更全面的分析。我们对Gaia DR3星表中最佳拟合附近的星象抖动以及Hipparcos与Gaia之间的自行异常进行了分析，从而对轨道倾角施加了强有力的约束。我们利用所有可用的CORALIE和HARPS光谱仪的径向速度数据进行了新的动力学拟合。我们使用嵌套抽样生成的样本评估了系统的动力学构型和长期稳定性。我们的分析表明，轨道倾角被强烈约束在约51°。因此，假设共面且倾斜（i=51°）的最佳拟合动力学解给出内次矮星质量为21.56 M_J，外巨行星质量为3.12 M_J。相应的轨道周期分别为256.26天和1298.87天，偏心率分别为0.426和0.180。对五个相关共振角的研究表明，只有一个共振角以大振幅摆动。我们的稳定性分析证实该系统是动力学稳定的。我们的结果为系统的质量和倾角提供了新约束，挑战了早前面向构型的说法，并揭示了系外行星系统中亚恒星伴星的形成与演化。

## Abstract
The HD 202206 system, which features two substellar companions with a 5:1 period ratio around a solar-type star, offers a rare opportunity to study high-order mean-motion resonances and provides new insights into the formation and evolution of substellar companions in extrasolar planetary systems. We revisited the HD 202206 system, aiming to conduct a more comprehensive analysis of orbital inclinations, companion masses, resonance dynamics, and potential formation mechanisms. Our analysis of the astrometric jitter around the best fit in the Gaia DR3 catalog and the proper motion anomalies between Hipparcos and Gaia places robust constraints on the orbital inclinations. We performed a new dynamical fit to all available radial velocity data from the CORALIE and HARPS spectrographs. We assessed the dynamical configuration and long-term stability of the system using samples generated by nested sampling. Our analysis shows that the orbital inclinations are strongly constrained to about $51^\circ$. Consequently, the best-fit dynamical solution, assuming a coplanar and inclined ($i = 51^\circ$) configuration, yields an inner brown dwarf of $21.56 M_\mathrm{J}$ and an outer giant planet of $3.12 M_\mathrm{J}$. The corresponding orbital periods are 256.26 days and 1298.87 days, with eccentricities of 0.426 and 0.180. An investigation of the five relevant resonance angles shows that only one is librating with a large amplitude. Our stability analysis confirms that the system is dynamically stable. Our results provide new constraints on the masses and inclination of the system, challenging earlier claims of a face-on configuration and shedding light on the formation and evolution of substellar companions in extrasolar planetary systems.
